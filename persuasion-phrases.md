## Phrase Replacement Guide

### Hedging & Uncertainty

| Weak Phrase | Powerful Alternative | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| "I think we should..." | "I recommend we..." | Shows conviction and positions you as decisive |
| "I think..." | "Based on my analysis..." | Grounds opinion in expertise |
| "I believe..." | "The evidence suggests..." | Shifts to objective authority |
| "I feel like..." | "My assessment is..." | Removes emotional hedging |
| "In my opinion..." | State the opinion directly | Your name is on it; ownership is implicit |
| "I guess..." | "My understanding is..." | Eliminates doubt |
| "Maybe we could..." | "Let's consider..." | Positions as active proposal |
| "I could be wrong, but..." | "One perspective is..." | Removes self-doubt while staying open |
| "I might be wrong..." | "Here's what I'm seeing..." | Confident observation |
| "I may be wrong..." | "Based on what we know..." | Grounds in facts |

### Apologies & Self-Deprecation

| Weak Phrase | Powerful Alternative | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| "Sorry to bother you..." | "I wanted to follow up on..." | You're not a bother; you're doing your job |
| "Sorry, but..." | Remove entirely or replace with "However..." | Apologizing diminishes your position |
| "I'm no expert, but..." | "Based on my experience..." | You don't need to disclaim your knowledge |
| "This might be a stupid question, but..." | "Quick question:" or "To clarify:" | No question that helps you is stupid |
| "This might be a silly idea, but..." | "One approach to consider:" | Let others judge the idea on its merits |
| "Forgive me for asking..." | "I'd like to understand..." | Curiosity doesn't require forgiveness |
| "I'm just saying..." | Remove entirely | You said it; we know |

### Qualifiers & Minimizers

| Weak Phrase | Powerful Alternative | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| "Just wanted to..." | "I'm writing to..." | "Just" shrinks your power |
| "Just checking in..." | "Following up on..." | Direct and purposeful |
| "Just my two cents..." | "My recommendation is..." | Your input has value |
| "I just think..." | "I recommend..." | Remove the minimizer |
| "Actually, I..." | Remove "actually" | Communicates surprise that you have input |
| "Kind of..." | Remove or be specific | Vague qualifiers weaken statements |
| "Sort of..." | Remove or be specific | Same as above |
| "A little bit..." | Quantify specifically | Precision shows confidence |
| "Basically..." | Remove or explain fully | Either explain or don't |
| "Honestly..." | Remove | Implies you're not normally honest |
| "To be honest..." | Remove | Same as above |
| "Literally..." | Remove (unless truly literal) | Often misused; sounds juvenile |
| "Very..." | Use a stronger word | "Very good" â†’ "excellent" |
| "Really..." | Use a stronger word | Same as above |

### Vague Commitments

| Weak Phrase | Powerful Alternative | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| "I'll try to..." | "I will..." or "I'll have this done by..." | Commit or explain constraints |
| "I should be able to..." | "I will..." with specific timeline | Shows accountability |
| "Hopefully..." | "The plan is..." or "We expect..." | Hope isn't a strategy |
| "I was wondering if maybe..." | "Would you be open to..." | Direct request |
| "We're basically going to maybe try..." | "Our plan is..." | Clear and decisive |

### Seeking Validation

| Weak Phrase | Powerful Alternative | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| "Does that make sense?" | "Let me know if you have questions" | Don't question your own clarity |
| "Did that make sense?" | "I'm happy to elaborate if helpful" | Same as above |
| "If that makes sense..." | "To summarize..." | Own your explanation |
| "If that's okay..." | "Please let me know your thoughts" | You've made a proposal; await response |
| "Right?" (seeking agreement) | Pause instead | Let your statement stand |
| "You know?" | Remove | Filler that seeks validation |

### Follow-ups & Requests

| Weak Phrase | Powerful Alternative | Why It Matters |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------|
| "Just following up..." | "Following up on [specific item]. Could you provide [specific need] by [date]?" | Clear, purposeful, time-bound |
| "Hope you're well..." (email opener) | Get to the point, or use "I hope this message finds you well" once | Overused; delays your message |
| "Happy to help with whatever you need" | "I'd be glad to support [specific area]. What would be most useful?" | Shows intentionality |
| "When you get a chance..." | "By [specific date]..." | Creates urgency and clarity |
| "No worries if not..." | Remove or be direct about flexibility | Undermines your request |
| "Feel free to..." | "Please..." | More direct |

## Words to Eliminate

### Filler Words (Verbal)
Remove from speech entirely:
- Um, uh, er
- Like (as filler)
- You know
- So (as starter)
- Basically
- Actually
- Literally

### Undermining Words (Written & Verbal)
- Just
- Sorry (when not genuinely apologizing)
- Kind of / Sort of
- Maybe
- I think / I feel / I believe (overuse)
- Honestly / To be honest
- Very / Really

### Excessive Punctuation
- !!! (Multiple exclamation points)
- ... (Overuse of ellipses)

## Power Phrases to Adopt

### Making Recommendations
- "I recommend..."
- "My recommendation is..."
- "The best course of action is..."
- "Based on my analysis, we should..."
- "I propose..."
- "Let's move forward with..."

### Expressing Certainty
- "I'm confident that..."
- "This will..."
- "We can expect..."
- "The data shows..."
- "Based on our findings..."

### Setting Boundaries
- "That won't work for me because..."
- "I can commit to X by Y date"
- "My priority right now is..."
- "I need to decline this time"

### Taking Ownership
- "I'll take care of that"
- "I'll own this"
- "You can count on me for..."
- "I'll have this to you by..."

### Disagreeing Professionally
- "I see it differently..."
- "Another perspective to consider..."
- "Let me offer an alternative view..."
- "I'd push back on that because..."

### Asking Questions
- "Help me understand..."
- "What's driving that decision?"
- "Can you walk me through the reasoning?"
- "What would need to be true for...?"

### Closing Conversations
- "Here are the next steps..."
- "Let's align on..."
- "To confirm, we're moving forward with..."
- "I'll follow up by [date] with..."

## Communication Contexts

### In Meetings
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "I just have a quick thought..." | "I'd like to add..." |
| "Sorry to interrupt, but..." | "Building on that..." or simply start speaking |
| "This might not be relevant, but..." | "One consideration is..." |
| "I'm not sure if this is right..." | "My take is..." |

### In Emails
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "I wanted to reach out..." | "I'm reaching out to..." |
| "I was hoping you could..." | "Could you please..." |
| "Sorry for the delay..." | "Thank you for your patience..." |
| "Let me know if you need anything else!" | "I'm available for follow-up questions." |

### In Negotiations
| Instead of... | Say... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Would you maybe consider..." | "I propose..." |
| "I was thinking something around..." | "The rate is..." |
| "If it's not too much trouble..." | "I'll need..." |

## Quick Reference Card

### Before you speak/write, ask:
1. Am I apologizing unnecessarily?
2. Am I using "just" to minimize my message?
3. Am I hedging with "I think" or "I believe"?
4. Am I seeking validation with "Does that make sense?"
5. Am I committing with "I'll try" instead of "I will"?

### The 5-Second Reframe:
**Weak:** "I just wanted to check in and see if maybe you had a chance to look at my proposal? Sorry to bother you!"

**Strong:** "Following up on my proposal from Tuesday. I'd appreciate your feedback by Friday so we can move forward."
